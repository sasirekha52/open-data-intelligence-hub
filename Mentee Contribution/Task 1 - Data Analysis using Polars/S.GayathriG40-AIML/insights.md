# Task 1 - Data Analysis Insights (Polars)

## Overview
This analysis is based on the World Happiness Report dataset, which evaluates countries using multiple quality-of-life indicators such as GDP, social support, life expectancy, freedom, generosity, and corruption perception.

---

## Key Insights

### 1. GDP and Happiness
Countries with higher GDP per capita generally have higher life evaluation scores, showing that economic strength strongly influences happiness.

---

### 2. Social Support Matters
Social support is one of the strongest contributing factors to happiness. Countries with strong community and family systems tend to rank higher.

---

### 3. Healthy Life Expectancy
Higher life expectancy is closely linked with better happiness rankings, indicating that healthcare quality plays a major role in quality of life.

---

### 4. Freedom to Make Life Choices
Countries where people feel more freedom in decision-making show higher happiness scores compared to those with restrictions.

---

### 5. Corruption Perception
Higher perception of corruption negatively affects happiness scores. Countries with lower corruption levels tend to rank better.

---

### 6. Generosity
Generosity has a weaker but still positive relationship with happiness scores.

---

## Conclusion
Overall, happiness is not driven by a single factor. It is a combination of economic stability, social support, health conditions, and governance quality.
